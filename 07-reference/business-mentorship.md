# Reference: Business Mentorship & Coaching Integration

*How coaching sessions, business strategy, and mentorship feedback get processed into operational improvements.*

---

## The Setup

Cordell works with Hugh Francis (garden3d / Sanctuary Computer) as his primary business coach. Hugh built Sanctuary Computer into a respected digital studio through relentless content publishing, community building, and thoughtful positioning. The coaching relationship covers sales strategy, positioning, content/thought leadership, operational excellence, and mindset.

The coaching sessions happen weekly (roughly). Each session produces 50-70K characters of raw transcript via Granola. But the value isn't in the transcript — it's in how the insights get processed, operationalized, and compounded across sessions.

---

## How Alice Processes Coaching Sessions

### The Workflow

1. **Capture**: Granola records the call automatically. Alice pulls the transcript via MCP API.
2. **Deep analysis**: Process the transcript against full business context — current sprint, cash flow, active deals, recent decisions.
3. **Structured notes**: Write `memory/meetings/YYYY-MM-DD-coaching-{coach}.md` with themes, action items, key quotes, strategic insights.
4. **Notion tasks**: Create tasks with specific structure for coaching homework.
5. **Telegram summary**: Send Cordell the top takeaways and Notion link.

### Notion Task Structure for Coaching Homework

```
📋 Coaching Homework — [Date]
├── Mindset Shifts
│   └── Strategic reframes to internalize
├── Outreach/Sales
│   └── Specific people to contact, messaging approaches
├── Content/Positioning
│   └── Writing, thought leadership, case study updates
├── Operational Changes
│   └── Process adjustments, team management, tool changes
├── Key Quotes
│   └── Verbatim quotes for reference
└── Next Session Prep
    └── What to report back, questions to raise
```

## Key Frameworks from Coaching

### Big Mario Marketing
Hugh's framework for positioning: don't sell the flower (your services), sell the transformation (what the client becomes with the flower).

> "You're selling them the vision of the future of them, not selling them the thing that they have to eat."

**How Alice applies this**: When drafting pitch materials or case studies, Alice frames outcomes as transformations, not deliverables. Not "we designed their merchandise" but "we built a merchandise program that generated $X in revenue and created a new customer acquisition channel."

**Referenced exemplars:**
- Beck & Beck — outcome sections on case studies showing before/after transformation
- Fictive Kin — "Your website owes you money" content that sells the outcome (revenue) not the service (web development)

### Incessant Publishing
Hugh's growth engine at Sanctuary Computer: relentless content production and thought leadership as the primary lead generation channel.

> "We are just incessant and do not stop publishing and writing thought leadership... we just always have leads coming through the door."

> "Early days of Sanctuary Computer was just me writing on Medium on weekends."

**How Alice applies this**: Content creation is treated as a first-class operational priority, not a "when we have time" activity. The articles in this documentation, the Twitter threads, the positioning pieces — they're all outputs of this framework.

### VA/EA Performance Standards
Hugh demonstrated his own EA's daily standup reports (Christian) as the gold standard for assistant management.

> "I wouldn't settle for anything less than my assistant. He's fucking amazing."

> "They should bring more solutions than problems. Every day."

**Standard:** Daily end-of-shift standup reports. Tasks sitting untouched for 2 weeks with no communication is a firing offense. Escalate to account manager immediately.

### Cultural Bridge Positioning
Hugh pushed Cordell to own the hybrid positioning — not traditional ad agency, not dev shop, but the agency that "gets it" for the current moment.

> "You have this opportunity by saying Superculture is not a traditional ad agency... because the industry is changing, and here's why we think it's changing."

> "The agency that gets it. That gets all of this that's happening at the current time."

**The insight**: What's obvious to Cordell (tech Twitter meta, cultural capital, AI-native operations) is NOT obvious to the broader market. There's real arbitrage in taste-level cultural translation.

## How Coaching Compounds Through Alice

### Session-Over-Session Continuity
Before each coaching call, Alice prepares a briefing:
- Review all previous coaching notes
- Status of homework from last session (completed, in progress, blocked)
- New developments since last call
- Suggested topics for this session

This means the coaching relationship never loses context. Hugh doesn't need to re-establish what was discussed two weeks ago — Alice already has it prepared.

### Framework Integration
Coaching frameworks don't just get documented — they get operationalized:

| Framework | Where It Shows Up |
|-----------|------------------|
| Big Mario Marketing | Pitch deck structure, case study templates, email drafts |
| Incessant Publishing | Content calendar, Twitter thread generation, article drafts |
| VA Standards | Daily standup templates, performance tracking |
| Cultural Bridge | Positioning docs, ICP definition, cold outreach messaging |

### Cross-Session Pattern Recognition
Over multiple coaching sessions, patterns emerge that single sessions don't reveal:
- Recurring themes Hugh returns to (usually means Cordell hasn't internalized it yet)
- Strategic shifts in emphasis (from "get any revenue" to "get the right revenue")
- Evolution of positioning language (tracked across sessions to see what sticks)

Alice's weekly synthesis cron picks up these patterns and flags them.

## Why This Matters for Alice

The coaching integration demonstrates a key capability of the AI Chief of Staff model: **external expertise gets captured, operationalized, and compounded automatically.**

A founder who meets with a coach weekly but doesn't systematically capture and implement the advice is wasting 80% of the coaching value. The transcript gets forgotten. The homework doesn't get tracked. The frameworks don't get applied.

With Alice processing every session:
- Nothing from the conversation is lost
- Every action item becomes a tracked task
- Every framework becomes an operational pattern
- Every insight gets cross-referenced against the full business context
- Session continuity is maintained automatically

> Coaching is an investment. The ROI depends on implementation. Alice ensures that every coaching dollar translates into captured insights, tracked homework, operationalized frameworks, and compounding business intelligence. The coach's value multiplies because nothing falls through the cracks.
