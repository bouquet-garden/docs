# How We Screened 1,167 Creative Director Candidates in Days, Not Months

Hiring a Creative Director is one of the hardest calls a studio makes. It's not about checking boxes — it's about taste, cultural fluency, and the ability to think conceptually at speed. The kind of evaluation that usually takes a senior partner weeks of portfolio reviews, gut-feel conversations, and reference calls.

We ran the entire process through Alice — from ingesting 1,167 raw applications to producing deep behavioral analysis on 6 finalists. Two made it to paid trials. The whole funnel took days, not the months it would have taken a traditional recruiter.

## The Scale Problem

The job post went wide. 1,167 candidates applied through a Notion form that captured:

- **Resume PDF** — work history, titles, tenure
- **Portfolio / Website URL** — the actual work
- **~5-minute Loom video** — how they think, present, and communicate
- **LinkedIn, Instagram/Twitter** — social proof and cultural presence
- **Self-reported strengths** — multi-select from 20+ creative disciplines
- **Timezone, availability, engagement preference** — logistics

This isn't a job board with a text field and an "Upload Resume" button. Every candidate submitted multi-modal signal: documents, video, links, and self-assessment. The richness is the point — and the problem. No human team can meaningfully evaluate 1,167 multi-signal applications.

## What Alice Actually Did

This wasn't keyword matching. This wasn't "does the resume say Figma." Alice performed deep qualitative assessment across every signal type:

1. **Resume parsing** — extracted career arcs, title progression, brand-name experience, cross-disciplinary range
2. **Portfolio scraping** — visited every portfolio URL, analyzed the actual work for quality, range, and conceptual depth
3. **Loom transcript extraction** — pulled and analyzed video transcripts for communication style, strategic thinking, and self-awareness
4. **Structured multi-signal evaluation** — synthesized all three inputs into a unified candidate profile

The key distinction: Alice wasn't filtering *out*. She was filtering *for* — looking for the specific combination of taste, strategic thinking, and cultural fluency that defines a great Creative Director.

Of the 128 candidates who received detailed ratings: 66 were rated average, 43 below average, 8 poor, and 11 good. The remaining 1,039 were screened out before deep evaluation — missing critical signals, misaligned experience, or insufficient portfolio depth.

**That's a 99.5% filtering rate** — 1,167 down to 6 — with structured reasoning at every cut.

## The Evaluation Framework

For the 11 "Good" candidates and the 6 finalists, Alice applied a structured 5-dimension scoring system. Each dimension scored out of 5, for a total of 25:

### 1. Archive (/5)
Is their body of work organized, substantial (5+ years), cross-disciplinary, and does it tell a story? A Creative Director's archive *is* their argument for why they should lead creative.

### 2. Skill Gap (/5)
How close are they to the specific capabilities we need — merchandise development, illustration, pitch deck creation? Not disqualifying, but mapped precisely.

### 3. Cultural Fluency (/5)
Do they live in culture? Niche interests, subcultural awareness, the ability to reference beyond the obvious. This is the difference between someone who follows trends and someone who *sees* them forming.

### 4. Taste Alignment (/5)
Is their work elevated, memorable, boundary-pushing? Do they make the kind of decisions we'd make — or better, the kind we wish we'd made?

### 5. Collaboration Signals (/5)
How do they talk about working with teams? Do they credit collaborators? Do they describe process or just output?

**Our top-scoring finalist hit 24/25** — a candidate with experience at major fashion and lifestyle brands, showing extraordinary range across art direction, brand identity, and product design.

## The "Taste Can't Be Trained" Principle

The entire evaluation was built around a core hiring philosophy:

> *Concept building and strategic thinking is the rarer, higher-leverage skill. Technical gaps can be supported with junior designers. Taste and conceptual ability cannot be trained.*

This is why the framework weights Cultural Fluency and Taste Alignment so heavily. You can teach someone a new tool in a week. You can't teach them to have interesting references or to know when something feels *right*.

Alice operationalized this philosophy at scale — something that's nearly impossible with traditional recruiting, where time pressure forces evaluators to over-index on hard skills and keyword matches.

## Deep Analysis: Beyond the Score

Each finalist received a **Recruiter Deep Analysis** — not just a number, but a behavioral profile:

- **Green flags** — signals of excellence, standout moments in their portfolio or Loom
- **Yellow flags** — areas worth probing in interviews, potential gaps that aren't dealbreakers
- **Red flags** — concerns that need direct answers
- **Industry meta-context** — how their experience maps to the current creative landscape, what their career trajectory suggests about their ambitions

This is the kind of analysis a great recruiting partner provides after weeks of relationship-building with a candidate. Alice produced it from first-pass materials in minutes.

## What This Means

Traditional creative hiring at this scale has two failure modes:

1. **You filter too aggressively** — keyword matching, title screening, brand-name bias — and miss the best candidates who don't fit the template
2. **You filter too loosely** — drowning in portfolios, making gut calls after 30-second scans, and the process takes months

Alice eliminates both failure modes. Every candidate got genuine multi-signal evaluation. The ones who advanced earned it through demonstrated taste, strategic thinking, and cultural depth — not because they had the right keywords or went to the right school.

**1,167 → 6 → 2.** In days. With structured reasoning you can audit at every step.

That's not automation. That's judgment at scale.
